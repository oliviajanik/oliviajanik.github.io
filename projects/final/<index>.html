Petfinder data shines light on adoptable dogs in pre-pandemic America

by Olivia Janik

On September 20, 2019, there were 58,180 dogs available for adoption in the United States on the website Petfinder.

Founded in 1996, Petfinder is an online database of adoptable animals, from cats and dogs to horses and other barnyard animals. As the largest pet adoption website in North America, it partners with animal shelters and adoption organizations to provide users with information on thousands of adoptable animals all over the United States.

On Sept. 20, there were adoptable dogs available in all 50 states, ranging from 15 in Alaska—six German Shepards, eight Huskies and one Dachshund—to 4,002 dogs in New York.

Besides New York, the states with the most adoptable dogs were Georgia, Virginia, New Jersey and Pennsylvania, all with more than 2,500 dogs.

Peanut Butter, a Pit Bull rescue from Virginia (Petfinder).

Emily Hovermale works for Humane Rescue Alliance, an animal shelter based in Washington DC that has dogs featured on Petfinder. As someone who’s always had a passion for helping animals, Hovermale jumped at the opportunity to work for HRA.

In the DC metro area, the group works to connect the community with animals in need, as well as advocate for legislation that protects animals, Hovermale said.

“They can’t speak for themselves. They can’t protect themselves or advocate for themselves,” she said.

Petfinder allows smaller animal welfare organizations and shelters to broadcast their dogs to a larger audience,giving them a better chance of being adopted.

With so many dogs available for adoption across the country, it's important for people to consider adopting rather than buying a dog from a breeder, according to a Humane Rescue Alliance statement.

And while many believe that buying from a breeder is the only way to find a purebred dog, 28.5% of dogs available on Sept. 20, 2019, were purebred.

The most common breeds available on Petfinder were Pit Bull Terriers, with 7,890 available dogs and Labrador Retrievers, with 7,198 adoptable dogs.

For Dan Reed, who rescued a Pit Bull and loved his dog Drizzy so much that he decided to keep fostering other Pit Bulls, adopting is an incredibly rewarding experience. Learning about the group of breeds encouraged him to continue working with rescues for Pit Bulls because they are often judged harshly.

“I’ve really fallen in love with the breed,” Reed said. “They’re really awesome dogs.”

With so many Pit Bulls up for adoption, it’s important to work to end the stigma to ensure that these dogs find good homes, he said.

There are dogs of all ages on Petfinder available. While there are nearly 10,000 puppies that were available for adoption, older dogs also needed homes. The largest group was adult dogs with 27,955 ready to find a home, but many senior dogs needed homes too, with 4,634 looking to be adopted.

There are numerous rescues featured on Petfinder tailored towards senior dogs, a group that often gets overlooked because of their age, according to the website.

Almost three years after the data was collected and after a global pandemic, there were more than 100,000 dogs available for adoption on May 5, 2022, showing the ever-present need for new homes for animals in the United States.
